# What am I doing?

Interstitial journal app: a low‑friction “now” log that helps scattered people anchor attention by briefly naming the thing they’re doing and then going to do the thing.

## Concept

- Core loop: answer “What am I doing?” with a tiny, present‑tense step whenever you feel scattered; the app timestamps it and updates your “Now” state.
- Purpose: build gentle time/attention awareness without planners, streaks, or heavy structure; useful even with sporadic use.
- Audience: anyone who feels scattered (ADHD‑informed but generalized).
- Philosophy: tool as waypoint, not destination—log → see “Now” → go do the thing.

## Notes and behavior principles

- Forgiving usage: still valuable with 1–3 entries/day or gaps of days/weeks; no penalties, no streaks, no guilt.
- Single entry is a success: each log is a small win; lean into clarity and follow‑through, not gamification.
- Rewards tied to action: subtle feedback on logging, slightly stronger “did the thing” language on completion; no points, badges, or feeds.
- Copy tone: clear, calm, a bit friendly; phrases like “do the thing” / “did the thing” used sparingly and tied to real‑world action.

## What it’s not

- Not a planner or task manager. It doesn’t try to hold your whole life, schedule, or priorities.
- Not a habit tracker. No streaks, no points, no daily goals, no “keep it up” pressure.
- Not a journaling app for long reflection. Entries are intentionally tiny and present‑tense.
- Not a productivity dashboard. No analytics, charts, or optimization loops that replace doing the thing.
- Not a social feed. No sharing, likes, or performance.
- Not therapy or diagnosis. It can be ADHD‑informed, but it isn’t treatment and doesn’t claim to “fix” attention.
- Not a note‑taking system. Mood notes are optional context, not a second brain.

## Why?

- **Fast re‑orientation.** When attention fragments, naming the next tiny step creates a concrete foothold.
- **Low shame, high return.** Gaps are expected, so the tool stays usable instead of becoming another failing system.
- **Action‑biased.** The “Now” line is a waypoint that points back to the world: log → see → go.
- **Fewer decisions.** One prompt and one state reduces cognitive overhead compared to lists and plans.
- **Creates a gentle trail.** The timeline becomes lightweight evidence of what you actually did, without demanding consistency.
- **Optional emotion metadata.** Mood + note can surface patterns and context without turning into a full journal.

## Product stance

- No streaks, points, or badges; no punishments for gaps.
- Designed for quick drop‑in use: open → log → see “Now” → close → go do the thing.
- Logging aims to feel like a small, satisfying alignment moment that supports doing the thing, not a substitute for the thing itself.
